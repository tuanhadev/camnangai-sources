# Claude Opus 4.7 Is Here: Anthropic's Most Capable Model Yet

Everything you need to know about Anthropic's latest release, Opus 4.7, new features, benchmark results, and what's actually changed.

**By Emilyharbord | Apr 17, 2026 | 3 min read**

---

Anthropic just published Claude Opus 4.7, and it's not a minor update. With meaningful improvements across coding, vision, instruction following, and memory, this release sets a new bar for what Claude can do.

Here's everything you need to know.

## Coding Just Got Better in Claude Opus 4.7

Opus 4.7 scores 64.3% on SWE-bench Pro, one of the most demanding benchmarks for real-world software engineering. Unlike academic coding tests, SWE-bench Pro evaluates a model's ability to fix actual bugs in open-source projects, the kind of messy problems developers face every day.

Crossing the 64% threshold in this benchmark is a meaningful milestone. It signals that Opus 4.7 isn't just good at writing; it can reason through complex codebases and produce reliable fixes.

## Vision Upgrades in Opus 4.7: Sharper, More Detailed Analysis

Opus 4.7 brings a noticeably improved Vision capability with support for higher resolutions. In practice, this means:

- More accurate interpretation of charts, dashboards, and diagrams.
- Better reading of screenshot-based code and UI layouts.
- Finer detail recognition in technical and scientific images.

For developers building visual workflows or analysts working with data-heavy visuals, this upgrade alone is worth paying attention to.

## How Opus 4.7 Handles Complex Instructions Differently

Anthropic has put serious effort into making Opus 4.7 follow instructions more precisely. The model stays closer to your original prompt, respects formatting constraints more consistently, and handles long, multi-part instructions without losing track of earlier requirements.

If you've ever had a model drift from your instructions mid-response, you'll appreciate how much this matters in production environments, especially in agentic pipelines where a small deviation can fall into bigger problems.

## Claude Opus 4.7 Introduces the xhigh Effort Level

This is one of the more interesting additions. Opus 4.7 introduces a new xhigh effort level, giving developers granular control over how hard the model works on a given task.

With xhigh, you can tell Opus 4.7 to slow down and think harder on tough tasks, even if it takes a bit longer. This kind of compute-on-demand control is exactly what power users and enterprise teams have been asking for.

## Multi-Session Memory in Opus 4.7

Opus 4.7 now retains key context across sessions, remembering important details from previous conversations and carrying them forward. This brings Claude meaningfully closer to the experience of a persistent assistant that knows your projects, your preferences, and your working style, without requiring you to re-explain everything from scratch each time.

For teams using Claude continuously throughout the workday, this is a big improvement.

## How Opus 4.7 Compares to GPT-5.4 and Gemini 3.1 Pro

According to Anthropic, Opus 4.7 outperforms GPT-5.4 and Gemini 3.1 Pro across multiple benchmarks. In other words, Opus 4.7 is now one of the strongest AI models you can actually use.

Anthropic also admitted openly that Opus 4.7 doesn't yet match Mythos Preview, their more powerful model reserved for select enterprise partners. That kind of honesty is rare in the AI industry.

## Claude Opus 4.7 Pricing

Good news for existing users, pricing stays exactly the same as Opus 4.6:

- **Input:** $5 per million tokens
- **Output:** $25 per million tokens

You get a more capable model at the same cost. That's a straightforward win.

## Who Can Access Opus 4.7?

Opus 4.7 is live on Claude Page for all paid plan subscribers:

- Pro
- Max
- Team
- Enterprise

API access is also available, check Anthropic's official documentation for the model identifier and updated parameters.

## Final Thoughts

Claude Opus 4.7 delivers best improvements where they matter most: coding reliability, visual understanding, instruction precision, and conversational continuity. The addition of the xhigh effort level gives power users a meaningful new lever for demanding tasks.

If you're already on a paid Claude plan, there's no reason not to switch to Opus 4.7 today.
